Let's Get Started

I'm Ninjakittenz

and I'm currently still part of the population addicted to playing Pokemon Go. I made a list of items you'll need for the costume. I did things a little differently than other tutorials to try to keep my costume cheaper, but it still includes all of the pieces. The hat I bought off of Amazon. The shoes I got for around $20 at Target, but I know you can find black shoes with white soles for $10-15 at either Target or Walmart. The rest of the items I got from my local fabric store. I borrowed a sewing machine from someone, and I already had an iron and ironing board. These items aren't absolutely necessary, but they will make your life a lot easier.

What You Need

  • White baseball cap
  • Black gloves
  • Black tights
  • Black shoes with white soles
  • Zippers:
    • Blue - invisible (~20 inches)
    • White - separating (~6-10 inches)
  • Blue fabric (3 yards)
  • White fabric (1 yard)
  • Light grey fabric (1/4 yard)
  • Dark grey fabric (1/4 yard)
  • Dark blue fabric marker
  • White ribbed tank top
  • Yellow ribbon (for the shoe)
  • 1 circular thin piece of wood (for the belt buckle)
  • Yellow and white acrylic paint
  • 1" sew-on snap
  • Blue, light grey, and white clothing thread
  • Hot glue gun
  • A beginner's sewing kit (or sewing supplies such as a measuring tape, pins, and thread cutter)

The Hat

I bought the hat for about $5 off of Amazon. It's all white so it's completely customizable for the hat you need to make. I took pieces of paper and cut outlines of the triangles that make up the hat, made sure they fit nicely with some pins, then cut the fabric. For my hat, I needed just 2 different triangles. I was able to flip the triangles to fit each side of the hat. The next part is a little tricky.

I took each triangle of fabric and just hot glued them onto the hat. It sounds simple, but I definitely made a couple mistakes in the process. What made it easy for me was to hot glue the top corner of the fabric to the hat first, align the rest of the fabric, then hot glue the rest of the hat on. It's easy to make little bumps in the fabric, so be sure to put your hand inside of the hat to push the hat outward into it's curved shape. I also did the farthest left side and worked my around from there.
